Most tax software comparisons treat all filers the same. Self-employed individuals and freelancers have specific needs: Schedule C support, self-employment tax calculation, home office deductions, and quarterly tax tools. Here is an honest breakdown of which platforms handle these well in 2025 — and which charge the most for the same features.

ClearTaxGuide has no paid relationship with any tax software company. Pricing figures are verified from platform websites and independent reviews as of May 2026 and may change during filing season.

1. What self-employed filers actually need

These are the features that matter specifically for Schedule C filers:

  • Schedule C support — required for all freelance and self-employment income
  • Self-employment tax calculation — the 15.3% SE tax plus income tax
  • Home office deduction — both simplified and actual expense methods
  • 1099-NEC import — to pull contractor income in automatically
  • Quarterly tax estimator — to plan payments throughout the year

All four platforms below support these features. The real difference is how much they charge you for them.

7. Which should you choose?

Simple decision framework:

Sole proprietor or single-member LLC with straightforward income → FreeTaxUSA (save $100+)

Want hand-holding + live CPA access + complex investments → TurboTax Premium

Want in-person office backup option → H&R Block

Have an S-corp or multi-member LLC → TaxAct or hire a CPA

8. Frequently asked questions

What is the best free tax software for self-employed filers?
FreeTaxUSA offers completely free federal filing including Schedule C. State returns cost $15.99. For most freelancers and sole proprietors, it handles every Schedule C scenario at no federal cost.
Does TurboTax free version include Schedule C?
No. TurboTax's free tier does not include Schedule C. You must upgrade to TurboTax Premium ($129–$150 federal) to report self-employment income.
Should I use tax software or a CPA as a freelancer?
Tax software works well for most freelancers with straightforward Schedule C income. Hire a CPA if your net profit exceeds $100,000, you are considering an S corp election, or have complex multi-state situations. A CPA typically costs $250–$600 for a self-employed return.
Can I file for free as a self-employed person?
Yes. FreeTaxUSA offers free federal filing including Schedule C. If your AGI is $84,000 or below, you may also qualify for IRS Free File at irs.gov/freefile.
